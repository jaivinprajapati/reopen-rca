Bugs are NOT driving the reopen rise.
• Bug B (post-closure reopens) grew 5.7× in absolute terms (9 → 51/wk) and appears as a real, small ongoing tech problem — with a specific spike on Mar 30 – Apr 5 (135 bugs, 14% of reopens that week). This is your Apr bug, confirmed in the data.
• But even during that Apr-bug week, 86% of reopens were legitimate. Across the recent 3 weeks average, bugs are only 5.7% of reopens.
• Bug A (post-48h pre-closure) is 0 across every week — this class doesn't exist in the data, at least not as captured by resolution_to_reopening. Either those reopens always get captured as post-closure instead (i.e. the auto-close fires before the reopen), or this specific bug signature doesn't occur.
Caveat on Bug B source reconciliation: An independent query on dynamodb.tickets (raw Kapture status transitions, CLOSED/RESOLVED → OPEN with gap >48h) detected a ~1,200–1,500-event Feb bug spike concentrated Feb 3–16, far larger than what times_reopened_postclosure shows in service_ticket_model (63 peak week). The two flags capture different things; times_reopened_postclosure is the conservative, ticket-level flag we trust for this analysis. The raw-event query is a good secondary signal but can double-count state oscillations.
Conclusion — the real problem is legit reopens, up 128%.
The 2.07% → 5.34% rate rise is driven almost entirely by legitimate customer-initiated reopens inside the 48h window. Customers are hitting "not satisfied" at ~2.3× the rate they did in December. This is an operational fix-quality problem, not a tech defect. The RCA should now go into Px vs Cx queue decomposition on the legit-reopen bucket.

Two distinct failure patterns, mirroring TAT RCA Tree 1 / Tree 2.
• Cx collapsed. Near-perfect in Dec (0.81%) → 7.84% now — ~10× jump. This is the same "collapsed from near-perfect" signature TAT found in the Cx queue. Whatever broke there is also showing up here. Cx drove 23% of the overall rate rise despite being only ~11% of volume.
• Px degraded. 2.25% → 5.04% (~2.2×) — a serious regression but far less dramatic. But Px dominates volume (89%), so it's 77% of the overall rise by absolute impact.
Three observations worth carrying into the drill-down:
1. Cx volume dropped ~22% (2,293 → 1,799). Fewer CS tickets being raised — either genuine deflection or a routing change moving tickets into Px. Worth confirming before treating this as good news.
2. Cx peaked Feb 9 (10.23%) one week after Px peaked Feb 2 (7.53%). Suggests Feb bug hit Cx workflow 7 days after it hit Px — or Cx is the downstream effect of Px breakages.
3. Cx has not recovered nearly as much as Px post-Feb. Px settled to 5% (2.2× its 2.25% baseline). Cx settled to 7.8% (9.7× its 0.81% baseline). Cx's "new normal" is much further from its baseline — it's structurally worse.
